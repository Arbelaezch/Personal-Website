No Country for Old Men (2009)


Violence and mayhem ensue after a hunter stumbles upon a drug deal gone wrong and more than two million dollars in cash near the Rio Grande.

Directors: Ethan Coen, Joel Coen

Writers: Joel Coen, Ethan Coen

Stars: Tommy Lee Jones, Javier Bardem, Josh Brolin

Country: America

Producer: Richard Attenborough

Studio: Miramax Films



-won four awards at the 80th Academy Awards – Best Picture, Best Director(s), Best Supporting Actor (Bardem) and Best Adapted Screenplay -Based on No Country for Old Men by Cormac McCarthy -Roger Deakins -The tension is incredible. Bardem makes an impression every second he's on screen. -As is tradition with the Coens, each conversation is engaging. There's always something off just enough to make the characters hilariously grounded. -The hilarious depictions of everyday people set on the backdrop of a cold, tense, calculated game of cat and mouse is a perfect mirror for the message of the film. The message that life, just like this film, is permeated by a hard logical judgment, as well as absured randomness.
