Up (2009)

10/10



78-year-old Carl Fredricksen travels to Paradise Falls in his house equipped with balloons, inadvertently taking a young stowaway.

Director: Pete Docter

Writer: Pete Docter

Actors: Edward Asner, Jordan Nagai, John Ratzenberger









Upon initial release Up was met with insane critical and commercial success, cementing itself as one of The Greats within Pixar's, at the time, spotless resume of classics. Of course since 2009 Pixar has gone from consistently releasing films that pioneer and reinvent the Animated genre to putting out far less innovative works that are often sequels to prior franchises. These works, while never the bottom of the barrel of animated entertainment, are far from the impressive heights that Pixar was previously attaining on a yearly basis. This tainting of Pixar's good name has shown doubt on even their greatest hits as edgy film critics claim Pixar was "never that good." Apart of these cynics' opinions is often that the only reason Up is good is because of the opening ten minutes. I am here to convince you that these people could not be more wrong.
