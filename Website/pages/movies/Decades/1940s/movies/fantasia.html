Fantasia (1940)

Enjoyment:
Appreciation:


A collection of animated interpretations of great works of Western classical music.

Directors: James Algar, Samuel Armstrong

Writers: Joe Grant, Dick Huemer

Stars: Leopold Stokowski, Deems Taylor, The Philadelphia Orchestra

Country: America

Producer: Walt Disney

Studio: Walt Disney Productions and RKO Radio Pictures



-The animation and ideas are beautiful. -I think it's an enjoyable way to experience music, and a great way of using the power of both Film and Music to enhance the individual experience of both. -While acclaimed by critics, it was initially unable to make a profit due to World War II cutting off distribution to the European market, the film's high production costs, and the expense of leasing theatres and installing the Fantasound equipment for the roadshow presentations.
