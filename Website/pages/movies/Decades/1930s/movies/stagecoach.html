Stagecoach (1939)

Enjoyment:
Appreciation:


A man from a family of rich snobs becomes engaged to a woman from a good-natured but decidedly eccentric family.

Director: Frank Capra

Writers: Robert Riskin, George S. Kaufman

Stars: Jean Arthur, James Stewart, Lionel Barrymore

Country: America

Studio: Columbia Pictures



-Won Best Picture and Director for Capra -Capra's 3rd best director award in 5 years -Based on the Pulitzer prize winning play of the same name. -Very innocent. -James Stewart is just excellent -A little too innocent and playful for me -The comedy/romance doesnt hold and now even the narrative is just too sappy and positive. -Far too little Stewart. The focus is laid on the family, but theyre just way too cheery it nearly makes you sick. Of course its hard to write that sentence because it feels like insulting a child.
