Duck Soup (1933)

Enjoyment: 6

Appreciation: 5


Rufus T. Firefly is named president/dictator of bankrupt Freedonia and declares war on neighboring Sylvania over the love of wealthy Mrs. Teasdale.

Director: Leo McCarey

Writers: Bert Kalmar, Harry Ruby

Stars: Groucho Marx, Harpo Marx, Chico Marx, Zeppo Marx

Country of Origin: America

Studio: Paramount Pictures

A classic Marx Brother's comedy that ended up a box office disappointment.

- Marx Brothers comedy - Box office disappointment, but still a successful movie. Was released during the Depression, so people were a little more cynical. - Oddly also a musical. Also the earliest first fourth wall break I've seen since Great Train Robbery. - It’s a war comedy, yet the Marx Bros don't try to include any sort of political message. Just pure comedy. The comedy style really grew on me. Its like a fever pitch of comedy where nothing is really important or real, and anything can be true. Its so quick and wacky that you're basically always engaged with something.
